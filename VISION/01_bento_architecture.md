# 01 - The Bento Architecture

Paling's unit of work is a **Bento**. A Bento is a distinct directory containing the full lifecycle of a dataset, from raw human text to the final trained weights. Paling operates strictly on Bentos: a Bento is either complete (allowing `paling chat bentoname-buildid`) or it is in-progress (allowing status queries).

## Bento Components (Nomenclature)

To logically categorize the immense amount of data massaged for training, a Bento is composed of individual **Banchan** (components). Rather than assigning distinct names to every type of file, we simply refer to them as banchan within the context of their specific Bento.

Examples of Banchan inside a Bento:
*   **The Raw Text Banchan:** The unadulterated human text (e.g., the Sundarban story or the Wonder sigils).
*   **The Taxonometry Banchan:** The semantic signatures (Zipf averages, POS tags).
*   **The Kalman Banchan:** The algorithmic outputs tracking the conversational pointer.
*   **The Anchor Banchan:** The synthetic RLHF pairs generated by smaller models.
*   **The Output Banchan:** The final trained MLX LoRA adapters or fused GGUF weights.
